Introduction

In an era when the boundaries of science and technology are increasingly overlapping, the emergence of generative AI and large language models (LLMs) has marked a pivotal shift in scientific communication. These cutting-edge technologies offer unprecedented opportunities to enhance the dissemination of complex scientific knowledge, making it more accessible and understandable to diverse stakeholders. This workshop will explore how generative AI can promote Findability, Accessibility, Interoperability, and Reusability (FAIR) principles while upholding equity and inclusivity in scientific discourse.

Overview (Workshop Theme and Key Topics)

This workshop delves into the dynamic interplay between generative AI, including large language models (LLMs), and the FAIR principles and equity in science communication. It seeks to explore the advantages of using generative AI for spreading scientific knowledge and the potential pitfalls regarding diversity and data bias accompanying its use.

Relevance to the eScience Community

The proposed theme of Generative AI and LLM is relevant to the eScience community because of their overarching goal to help with scientific research through computation and data processing.

Expected Audience

The workshop is expected to attract a multifaceted audience comprising researchers, data scientists, AI specialists, and high-performance computing (HPC) experts.
